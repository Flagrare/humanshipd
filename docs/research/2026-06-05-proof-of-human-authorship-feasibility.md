# Research: Feasibility of Proving Human Authorship of Text

- **Slug:** `2026-06-05-proof-of-human-authorship-feasibility`
- **Date:** 2026-06-05
- **Status:** complete
- **Triggered by:** Scoping the "synthy-free" / "Authorshipped" concept — a tool to let a writer prove a piece of text was composed by a human, not generative AI (the inverse of DeepMind SynthID). Founder requested academic + industry research before design.
- **Informed:** Feasibility verdict and forthcoming design spec in `docs/superpowers/specs/` (cross-link to be added when written). Companion branding research: [`2026-06-05-human-authorship-label-framing.md`](./2026-06-05-human-authorship-label-framing.md).

## Question

Is it feasible to build a platform-agnostic, minimally-intrusive, privacy-respecting, OS-level tool that lets a writer **prove** a piece of written text was composed by a human and not generated by an LLM? Specifically: (1) what is the existing product/industry landscape; (2) do content-provenance standards (C2PA, SynthID, VCs) apply to human-authored text; (3) what process-attestation methodologies are empirically validated; (4) how adversarially robust is process attestation; (5) what can cryptography actually guarantee on user-controlled hardware; (6) what is technically and legally viable for cross-platform capture; and (7) is there a real policy hook (e.g. CBL, Authors Guild, KDP) that creates demand for a substantiation tool? Post-hoc "is this AI?" classifier detection (GPTZero/stylometry) was ruled out of scope by decision, documented below as to why.

### Academic — the core feasibility evidence

#### [On the Insecurity of Keystroke-Based AI Authorship Detection: Timing-Forgery Attacks Against Motor-Signal Verification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17280)
- **Authors / Org:** David Condrey (Writerslogic Inc.)
- **Type:** academic paper (cs.CR preprint — not peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2026-01-24 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the single most decisive source — critical/dissenting)
- **What this contributed:** Proves the adversarial ceiling for any timing-based approach. Formal **non-identifiability theorem**: mutual information between keystroke-timing features and content provenance, given the typed content and a motor model, is exactly zero — I(f(τ); Provenance | s, M) = 0 — via a data-processing-inequality argument (Provenance → Cognitive State → Motor Execution → timing). Empirically, the "copy-type" attack (human retypes LLM output) bypasses detectors **100%** across 879 transcription sessions / 11 corpora; three timing-forgery synthesizers (histogram, statistical, LSTM) achieve **≥99.8% evasion** using only public aggregate statistics. Conclusion that anchors our design: "this technology detects bots, not ghostwriters," and provenance verification requires signals *entangled with semantic content*.
- **Quoted:**
  > "this technology detects bots, not ghostwriters"

#### [Privacy-Preserving Proof of Human Authorship via Zero-Knowledge Process Attestation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00179)
- **Authors / Org:** David Condrey
- **Type:** academic paper (cs.CR preprint)
- **Published:** 2026-02-26 (v3 2026-05-23) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the design closest to our goal set)
- **What this contributed:** The academic blueprint nearest to synthy-free's requirements (OS-level, privacy-respecting, third-party-verifiable). Uses Groth16 ZK proofs + Pedersen commitments + Bulletproof range proofs + Poseidon Merkle/sequential-work-function chains at 30s checkpoints; verifier learns one bit per checkpoint; ~8.2 ms verify; claimed FAR < 10⁻¹². Explicitly states C2PA/VCs verify *source*, not *author type*. Honest limitations we would inherit: simulation-only (no real deployment), trusted-setup ceremony, ~23s proof-gen latency, assumes Gaussian typing distributions, and **does not defeat copy-type/blended attacks**. The "trust-inverted" framing (the attester is the adversary) is the core hard problem.

#### [Detecting Cognitive Signatures in Typing Behavior for Non-Intrusive Authorship Verification](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.00177)
- **Authors / Org:** David Condrey
- **Type:** academic paper (cs.CR preprint)
- **Published:** 2026 (v3 May 2026) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium-high (the steelman for what process data *can* show)
- **What this contributed:** Argues inter-keystroke-interval and pause dynamics distinguish ideation (composition) from retyping; supports the metadata-only, in-editor capture model and that keystroke dynamics carry authorship signal without storing content. Its own author's attack paper (2601.17280) shows the effect sizes collapse to security-irrelevance in free-text adversarial conditions (Cohen's d ≈ 0.14).

#### [A TEE-Based Architecture for Confidential and Dependable Process Attestation in Authorship Verification](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.00178)
- **Authors / Org:** David Condrey
- **Type:** academic paper (cs.CR preprint)
- **Published:** 2026 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium
- **What this contributed:** Hardware-trust variant making capture tamper-resistant; defends against client-side injection/middleware tampering but, like all process attestation, cannot bind ideas to typist — copy-type passes a TEE as readily as JS capture.

#### [Plagiarism Detection Using Keystroke Logs](https://educationaldatamining.org/edm2024/proceedings/2024.EDM-short-papers.47/2024.EDM-short-papers.47.pdf)
- **Authors / Org:** Scott Crossley, Y. Tian, J.S. Choi, L. Holmes, W. Morris (Vanderbilt University) — EDM 2024
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed conference)
- **Published:** 2024 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the optimistic case, with a crucial caveat)
- **What this contributed:** Random-forest model reached **99% accuracy** distinguishing authentic from transcribed essays — but only in a copy-paste-disabled "walled garden" with a non-adversarial typist. Authentic writing showed more pre-word/sentence pauses, more/longer insertions, more deletions, more revisions; transcription showed more continuous bursts and a linear trajectory. Establishes the composition-vs-copy signal is real *under controlled, trustworthy capture* — the boundary condition our design depends on.

#### [Using Keystroke Dynamics to Detect Nonoriginal Text](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jedm.12431)
- **Authors / Org:** Paul Deane (ETS)
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed, *Journal of Educational Measurement*) — abstract only, paywalled
- **Published:** 2026 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (independent confirmation from a measurement-science authority)
- **What this contributed:** Independently confirms keystroke process features discriminate original from reproduced text across a lab study plus two operational assessments, situating the signal in rigorous educational-measurement methodology.

#### [Keystroke Dynamics Against Academic Dishonesty in the Age of LLMs](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.15335v1)
- **Authors / Org:** D. Kundu, A. Mehta, R. Kumar, N. Lal, A. Anand, A. Singh, R.R. Shah
- **Type:** academic paper (arXiv preprint — not peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2024 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the realistic ceiling for the harder problem)
- **What this contributed:** Distinguishing bona-fide from *AI-assisted* writing (not mere pasting) achieved only **52–85% accuracy**, user-specific **FAR 23.7% / FRR 26.2%**, with severe cross-user generalization failure. Authors explicitly warn of wrongful-accusation risk. Defines the honest ceiling: detecting AI *assistance* from process data is not reliably validated.

#### [Spoofing keystroke dynamics authentication through synthetic typing pattern extracted from screen-recorded video](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40537-022-00662-8)
- **Authors / Org:** C.R.P. Siahaan, A. Chowanda (Bina Nusantara University) — *Journal of Big Data*
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2022 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (core spoofing evidence)
- **What this contributed:** Reconstructs a victim's keystroke timing from screen-recorded video to spoof keystroke-dynamics auth with **evasion up to ~64%** — even user-specific timing profiles are stealable. Shows behavioral signals are forgeable by a motivated adversary.

#### [QUACK! Making the (Rubber) Ducky Talk: A Systematic Study of Keystroke Dynamics for HID Injection Detection](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.15845v1)
- **Authors / Org:** A. Lotto, F. Marchiori, M. Conti (University of Padua)
- **Type:** academic paper (cs.CR preprint)
- **Published:** 2026-04-17 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium-high (the partial defense)
- **What this contributed:** Random Forest detects HID/scripted *injection* at ROC-AUC > 0.9 within ~70 keystrokes, and GAN sophistication does *not* improve evasion (defenders win by covering diverse attacker families). But it detects machine-vs-human *motor signal* — useless against a real-human copy-type adversary. Defines exactly which attack class is defensible (synthesis/injection) versus which is not (human transcription).

#### [Towards liveness detection in keystroke dynamics: Revealing synthetic forgeries](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2772941922000047)
- **Authors / Org:** *Cyber Security and Applications* (Elsevier) — full text 403-blocked, summarized from abstract
- **Type:** academic paper
- **Published:** 2022 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium
- **What this contributed:** Trains liveness classifiers on synthetic adversaries to flag forgeries — defends against synthesis, not against genuine human transcription. Reinforces the synthesis-vs-transcription defense boundary.

#### [Fluency in Writing: Generating Text in L1 and L2](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0741088301018001004)
- **Authors / Org:** N. Ann Chenoweth & John R. Hayes (Carnegie Mellon) — *Written Communication*
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2001 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium-high (theoretical foundation)
- **What this contributed:** Establishes the "burst/pause/revision" structure of human composition (text produced in bursts between planning pauses, tied to working memory in the Hayes model) — the principled, theory-grounded basis for the process feature set, rather than ad-hoc signals.

#### [Keystroke Logging in Writing Research: Using Inputlog to Analyze and Visualize Writing Processes](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0741088313491692)
- **Authors / Org:** Mariëlle Leijten & Luuk Van Waes (University of Antwerp) — *Written Communication*
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed) + tool (Inputlog)
- **Published:** 2013 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (canonical writing-analytics instrument)
- **What this contributed:** Documents how mature research tools capture pauses, P-/R-bursts, and revisions (standard 200ms motor / 2s planning thresholds) and produce replayable sessions. Crucially cautions that mapping log data to cognition is hard and recommends triangulation — a key honesty constraint for interpreting any process signal.

#### [Exploring the Feasibility of Using Writing Process Features to Assess Text Production Skills](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ets2.12071)
- **Authors / Org:** Paul Deane (ETS Research Report Series) — paywalled, summarized
- **Type:** academic report
- **Published:** 2015 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium-high
- **What this contributed:** Shows process features (burst length, pause location, revision rate) are measurable, valid constructs that vary with writer proficiency — grounding the feature taxonomy a tool would rely on.

#### [Proof of Humanity: A Multi-Layer Network Framework for Certifying Human-Originated Content](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.03752)
- **Authors / Org:** Sebastian Barros
- **Type:** academic paper (conceptual preprint)
- **Published:** 2025-04-02 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** low-medium (contrasting philosophy)
- **What this contributed:** Proposes telco/OSI-layer device-identity attestation (SIM/eSIM up to cryptographic attestations) instead of content analysis. Roadmap only, no implementation; useful as the "infrastructure not content" counter-philosophy and a reminder that content-agnostic approaches don't address text authorship.

#### [I can be you: Questioning the use of keystroke dynamics as biometrics](https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2013/) (NDSS 2013)
- **Authors / Org:** T.C. Meng, P. Gupta, D. Gao
- **Type:** academic paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2013 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium (foundational spoofability)
- **What this contributed:** Demonstrated 20–40% impersonation success against keystroke biometrics given a target profile — the long-standing position that keystroke timing is not a hard security boundary, which Condrey extends from identity to authorship.

### Standards & specifications — reusable building blocks

#### [C2PA Technical Specification v2.4 (Content Credentials)](https://spec.c2pa.org/specifications/specifications/2.4/specs/C2PA_Specification.html)
- **Authors / Org:** Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA) / Joint Development Foundation
- **Type:** spec (primary)
- **Published:** 2025 (v2.4) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the container to reuse)
- **What this contributed:** Confirms C2PA covers PDF 1.7/2.0, unstructured + structured text (Markdown/YAML/source/AsciiDoc), and ZIP-based docs (EPUB/OOXML/ODF). Manifest = claim (CBOR/JUMBF) + assertions + hard binding (hashes) + COSE/X.509 signature; text hard-binding via non-rendering Unicode or external sidecar manifest (`hashed-ext-uri-map`) — the sidecar path suits arbitrary text/platforms. Includes a `c2pa.ai-disclosure` assertion (disclose AI use) but **no human-authorship assertion** — the gap.

#### [C2PA launches Content Credentials 2.3](https://c2pa.org/the-c2pa-launches-content-credentials-2-3-and-celebrates-5-years-of-impact-across-the-digital-ecosystem/)
- **Authors / Org:** C2PA
- **Type:** vendor doc / announcement
- **Published:** 2026-02-09 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high
- **What this contributed:** v2.3 explicitly adds "plain text documents" to embeddable formats; 6,000+ members, live deployments — and notably still **no** human-authorship attestation feature, direct evidence of the standards gap.

#### [CAWG Identity Assertion v1.2](https://cawg.io/identity/1.2/)
- **Authors / Org:** Creator Assertions Working Group (CAWG), under Decentralized Identity Foundation
- **Type:** spec (primary)
- **Published:** 2025 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the authorship-binding mechanism — the key reusable piece)
- **What this contributed:** Lets a "named actor" cryptographically and non-repudiably bind identity to specific C2PA assertions independent of the producing tool. Supports role fields (`cawg.creator`, `cawg.contributor`, `cawg.editor`, etc.) and two credential types (`cawg.x509.cose`; `cawg.identity_claims_aggregation` carrying W3C VCs). Deliberately **silent on human vs. AI** (a named actor "may be human or non-human") — the exact extension point for a standardized "human-authored" claim.

#### [Verifiable Credentials Data Model v2.0](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/)
- **Authors / Org:** W3C
- **Type:** spec (primary)
- **Published:** 2025 (Recommendation track) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (attestation semantics)
- **What this contributed:** Standard for issuer-signed, third-party-verifiable claims; plugs into C2PA via CAWG `identity_claims_aggregation`. The mechanism by which a "this human authored this text" claim could be issued and verified across platforms.

#### [PROV-O: The PROV Ontology](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/) & [PAV: Provenance, Authoring and Versioning](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4177195/)
- **Authors / Org:** W3C (PROV-O); Ciccarese, Soiland-Reyes et al. (PAV)
- **Type:** spec (primary) / academic
- **Published:** 2013 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium (provenance vocabulary)
- **What this contributed:** PROV-O gives a domain-independent provenance model (entities/activities/agents) but is weak on authoring roles; PAV extends it to distinguish author/contributor/curator — the right semantics for expressing "human X authored this" in a provenance graph.

#### [Introducing SynthID Text](https://huggingface.co/blog/synthid-text)
- **Authors / Org:** Google DeepMind / Hugging Face (Nature paper s41586-024-08025-4)
- **Type:** vendor doc (+ peer-reviewed paper)
- **Published:** 2024-10-23 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (defines why the inverse approach can't prove human authorship)
- **What this contributed:** Watermarks *AI* text by perturbing token probabilities (g-function + Tournament sampling) **during `model.generate()`** — it requires the generating model at generation time and **cannot be applied retroactively to human-written text**. Confirms synthy-free is the conceptual inverse and that SynthID provides no building block. Fragile to paraphrasing; weak on short/factual text.

#### [PAdES — PDF Advanced Electronic Signature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAdES) (ETSI EN 319 142)
- **Authors / Org:** ETSI (standard); Wikipedia (summary)
- **Type:** spec summary
- **Published:** ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium (format-native signing)
- **What this contributed:** Long-term-validation signatures for PDFs, but PDF-only (DOCX uses OOXML signing) and proves *signer*, not human authorship or process — a secondary, format-specific delivery path, not the platform-agnostic core.

#### [Content Authenticity Initiative — How it works](https://contentauthenticity.org/how-it-works) ([open-source SDKs](https://opensource.contentauthenticity.org/docs/getting-started/))
- **Authors / Org:** Adobe / Content Authenticity Initiative
- **Type:** vendor doc
- **Published:** ongoing (CAI founded 2019) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium (implementation ecosystem)
- **What this contributed:** CAI is C2PA's implementation arm with open-source tooling (c2patool, SDKs). Image/video-centric today but built on the same C2PA core that now covers text — reusable infrastructure rather than something to rebuild.

### Cryptographic trust anchors

#### [RFC 3161: Internet X.509 PKI Time-Stamp Protocol](https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc3161.txt)
- **Authors / Org:** IETF
- **Type:** RFC/spec (primary)
- **Published:** 2001 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high
- **What this contributed:** The privacy-preserving "proof of existence before time T" primitive: client sends only a hash, the TSA appends trusted time and signs. Chaining many timestamps bounds a *sequence* of writing states over an interval — the strongest honest temporal claim. Proves an upper bound, not duration or a lower bound. Open TSAs exist ([sigstore/timestamp-authority](https://github.com/sigstore/timestamp-authority)).

#### [RFC 6962: Certificate Transparency](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc6962.html) & [Rekor](https://docs.sigstore.dev/logging/overview/)
- **Authors / Org:** IETF (CT); Sigstore (Rekor / [github](https://github.com/sigstore/rekor))
- **Type:** RFC/spec (primary); vendor/OSS doc
- **Published:** 2013 (CT); ongoing (Rekor) · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high
- **What this contributed:** Merkle-tree append-only logs with Signed Tree Heads, inclusion proofs, and consistency proofs make after-the-fact tampering/back-dating publicly detectable. Rekor provides this for arbitrary signed metadata. Solves public non-repudiation of a record's existence and ordering — but only if independently monitored ([Trail of Bits on transparency-log monitoring](https://blog.trailofbits.com/2025/12/12/catching-malicious-package-releases-using-a-transparency-log/)), so trust shifts to monitors, not the user.

#### [Key and ID Attestation (Android Open Source Project)](https://source.android.com/docs/security/features/keystore/attestation) & [Android key attestation](https://developer.android.com/privacy-and-security/security-key-attestation)
- **Authors / Org:** Google / AOSP
- **Type:** vendor doc (primary)
- **Published:** ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** critical (the cleanest statement of attestation's limits)
- **What this contributed:** Explicitly states attestation does **NOT** verify the provenance/authenticity of the data being signed, who/what created the input, or runtime integrity after attestation — only that key properties exist. The canonical "attests the container, not the content's human origin" source.

#### [App Attest & DeviceCheck (WWDC21)](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2021/10244/) & [Managed Device Attestation](https://support.apple.com/guide/deployment/managed-device-attestation-dep28afbde6a/web)
- **Authors / Org:** Apple
- **Type:** vendor doc / video
- **Published:** 2021 / ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high
- **What this contributed:** App Attest = Secure-Enclave key + Apple-signed attestation certifying genuine Apple device + unmodified app binary — the strongest consumer device anchor. By omission, it attests app/device, **not** human input: a genuine unmodified app fed synthetic/pasted input produces a perfectly valid attestation. Practical bypass surface noted by [Guardsquare](https://www.guardsquare.com/blog/remove-constraints-of-ios-app-attest).

#### [Foreshadow (USENIX Security 2018)](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/conference/usenixsecurity18/sec18-van_bulck.pdf) · [SGAxe](https://sgaxe.com/files/SGAxe.pdf) · [SoK: TrustZone/TEE Vulnerabilities (IEEE S&P 2020)](https://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/pfonseca/papers/sp2020-tees.pdf)
- **Authors / Org:** Van Bulck et al.; Van Schaik et al.; Cerdeira et al.
- **Type:** academic (peer-reviewed)
- **Published:** 2018 / 2020 / 2020 · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (TEE attestation is not unconditional)
- **What this contributed:** Foreshadow & SGAxe extracted SGX attestation keys, enabling forged "valid" remote-attestation quotes for code outside the enclave; SoK systematizes TrustZone weaknesses (software-implemented attestation in EL3, key reuse, missing rollback prevention). Even the hardware-trust layer degrades from "proof" to "raises cost/risk."

#### [Trusted path](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trusted_path) · [Trusted path / SAK (IBM AIX)](https://www.ibm.com/docs/en/aix/7.2.0?topic=security-trusted-path-trusted-shell-secure-attention-key) · [Secure Programming HOWTO §7.13](https://dwheeler.com/secure-programs/Secure-Programs-HOWTO/trusted-path.html)
- **Authors / Org:** IBM; David A. Wheeler
- **Type:** vendor doc / technical guide
- **Published:** ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (the theoretical limit)
- **What this contributed:** The "trusted path"/Secure Attention Key guarantees only that input reaches the genuine OS handler without a userspace spoof — it authenticates the *channel to the kernel*, not that a *human* generated the input. There is no cryptographic primitive signing "produced by a living human hand." [Frida](https://approov.io/knowledge/frida-detection-prevention) and the CAPTCHA collapse ([AI beats CAPTCHAs ~99.8%](https://futurism.com/the-byte/ai-better-solving-captchas-prove-human)) reinforce that human-vs-bot is statistical, never cryptographic.

### Cross-platform capture feasibility & legal/privacy

#### [CGEventTap in sandboxed apps](https://developer.apple.com/forums/thread/789896) · [CGEvent Taps and Code Signing: The Silent Disable Race](https://danielraffel.me/til/2026/02/19/cgevent-taps-and-code-signing-the-silent-disable-race/) · [Control input monitoring on Mac](https://support.apple.com/guide/mac-help/control-access-to-input-monitoring-on-mac-mchl4cedafb6/mac)
- **Authors / Org:** Apple Developer Forums / Apple Support; Daniel Raffel
- **Type:** vendor doc / blog
- **Published:** 2026 / ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (macOS feasibility)
- **What this contributed:** macOS global capture needs a `CGEventTap` gated by the TCC **Input Monitoring** permission; a listen-only tap stays in the less-scary Input-Monitoring bucket (vs Accessibility). Works in the sandbox / App Store with the `com.apple.security.device.input-monitoring` entitlement. But TCC binds to code identity, causing a documented *silent-disable race* after re-signing — "set and forget" reliability is hard, and capture can never be silent (user-visible, revocable).

#### [SetWindowsHookExA (winuser.h)](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/win32/api/winuser/nf-winuser-setwindowshookexa) · [Low-Level Windows Keylogger: Hooks to Evasion](https://dev.to/claudia-ve/building-a-low-level-windows-keylogger-in-c-from-hooks-to-evasion-5k9) · [Kaspersky: Implementing keyloggers in Windows](https://securelist.com/keyloggers-implementing-keyloggers-in-windows-part-two/36358/)
- **Authors / Org:** Microsoft Learn; Claudia V. (DEV); Kaspersky Securelist
- **Type:** vendor doc / blog (security vendor)
- **Published:** ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (Windows feasibility)
- **What this contributed:** `SetWindowsHookEx(WH_KEYBOARD_LL)` is *the* canonical keylogger primitive and heavily EDR/AV-monitored (quarantine/false-flag risk); Raw Input is lower-profile. Low-level hooks need a running message loop or Windows silently removes them. EV/Authenticode signing + SmartScreen reputation effectively mandatory; ships as a classic desktop installer, not a sandboxed Store app.

#### [X11 security / keylogging](https://dec05eba.com/2021/09/19/x11-security/) · [Wayland blocks key grabbing (RH Bugzilla #1285770)](https://bugzilla.redhat.com/show_bug.cgi?id=1285770) · [pynput on Wayland #628](https://github.com/moses-palmer/pynput/issues/628)
- **Authors / Org:** dec05eba; Red Hat Bugzilla; pynput / moses-palmer
- **Type:** blog / OSS bug tracker
- **Published:** 2021 / ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** high (Linux showstopper)
- **What this contributed:** X11 allows trivial unprivileged global capture (itself a known flaw); **Wayland deliberately blocks global input capture** (the hard showstopper) — XWayland only sees other XWayland clients; evdev needs root/`input` group; the InputCapture portal (libei/EIS) is incompletely implemented across compositors as of 2025. No portable, unprivileged, non-intrusive global capture exists on Linux.

#### Cross-platform hook libraries: [libuiohook](https://github.com/kwhat/libuiohook), [SharpHook](https://sharphook.tolik.io/), [pynput limitations](https://pynput.readthedocs.io/en/latest/limitations.html)
- **Authors / Org:** kwhat; Tolik Pylypchuk; pynput project
- **Type:** OSS / docs
- **Published:** ongoing · **Accessed:** 2026-06-05
- **Relevance:** medium-high
- **What this contributed:** These unify the API but inherit every platform showstopper (macOS grants, X11-only, Wayland unsupported/partial, AV-flagged Windows primitives) rather than solving any.

## Synthesis

**Verdict: Building a credible, useful tool is feasible. Building one that "proves" human authorship against a motivated adversary is not — and no one, academic or commercial, has done it.** The honest, defensible product is *tamper-evident attestation of a writing process*, framed as a credential that raises the cost of AI substitution and detects naive automation — never as cryptographic proof of human origin. Six load-bearing conclusions:

1. **The market is real but unsolved.** Products cluster into four camps: process-replay telemetry (Grammarly, Aidify, Originality, GPTZero, Turnitin, Draftback), behavioral-biometrics + crypto-seal (Purely Human, TypeOS — closest to us), trust/declaration certification (VerifiedHuman, Authors Guild), and infrastructure attestation (research only). Every shipping product is either spoofable telemetry or non-technical trust. "Prove human authorship" is genuinely open and defensible as a differentiator.

2. **Process data robustly separates *composition* from *transcription* — but only under cooperative/controlled capture.** The 99% accuracy result (Crossley/EDM 2024) holds because copy-paste was disabled and the typist wasn't adversarial. This is the scientific core synthy-free can stand on, and it is grounded in 20+ years of writing-process theory (bursts, planning pauses, revision).

3. **Against an adversary, process attestation is information-theoretically defeatable.** The copy-type attack (human retypes LLM output) yields genuine human motor signals — Condrey proves I(timing; provenance | content, motor) = 0, with 100% empirical bypass; blended composition is worse; off-the-shelf "human auto-typers" already forge revision history. Process signals can defend against *bots/injection* (QUACK, liveness detection at AUC > 0.9) but **never** against a human transcriber. Detecting AI *assistance* (vs. mere pasting) is unreliable (52–85%, FAR/FRR > 20%, wrongful-accusation risk).

4. **Cryptography solves the record, not the human.** Signatures + RFC 3161 timestamps + a transparency log (Rekor/CT-style) + hardware-backed keys/attestation make the record tamper-evident, time-anchored, append-only, and tied to a known device/app state — trust resting on PKI + monitors + hardware vendor, not the user's runtime. But the trusted path ends at the kernel, attestation ends at the binary/key, and the device owner sits on the analog hole. There is **no** "proof of humanity at the I/O layer," and even TEE attestation has real breaks (Foreshadow/SGAxe).

5. **Least-intrusive capture is the right architecture, and it's forced by the platforms anyway.** Global OS-level capture is defeated by Wayland, fragile under macOS TCC (silent-disable race), AV-flagged on Windows, and legally radioactive under GDPR (content logging → DPIA, possible Art. 9). The viable path: **per-surface capture** (dedicated editor, editor/IDE plugin, browser extension content script, embeddable SDK), capturing **metadata not content**, processed **on-device**, optionally wrapped in **zero-knowledge proofs** (Condrey ZK-PoP) to resolve the privacy-vs-attestation tension. The trade-off — no coverage for arbitrary apps — is the correct price for being non-intrusive and standards-clean.

6. **Don't reinvent the plumbing; ride the standards and contribute the missing claim.** C2PA manifests (now covering plain text) + CAWG identity assertions (which bind a named actor to assertions but are silent on human-vs-AI) + W3C VCs (PROV-O/PAV semantics) provide the container, signing, identity-binding, and verification layers. The white space synthy-free should own is the **standardized "human-authored" attestation/claim** plus the **process evidence** behind it. SynthID is the literal inverse and offers no building block.

7. **There is a concrete policy hook.** The CBL manual (May 2026), Amazon KDP's enforced disclosure, and especially the Authors Guild "Human Authored" mark — which openly admits it cannot verify authorship — create real demand for a substantiation tool. synthy-free's most defensible positioning is as the *evidence layer* behind an affirmative human-authored declaration, not a standalone "proof."

**Design implications carried forward:** (a) frame as attestation/credential, never "proof"; (b) capture per-surface, metadata-only, on-device; (c) bind content semantically (challenge-response, signed revision trajectories) to raise copy-type cost — while honestly disclosing copy-type as a residual, unsolved attack; (d) emit C2PA + CAWG + VC artifacts for third-party verification; (e) anchor records with signatures + RFC 3161 timestamps + a transparency log; (f) position against the Authors Guild / KDP / CBL disclosure regimes.
